Summary Of Born A Crime For Chapters 1 and 2

Trevor Noah was born in 1984 in South Africa during the apartheid era when relationships and marriages
between black and white people were illegal.His mother, Patricia, was a black Xhosa woman, and his father
was a white Swiss expatriate.Noah's very existence was considered a crime under the apartheid laws.


Noah describes how religion played a central role in his upbringing. His mother took the family to three
different churches every Sunday - an integrated megachurch, a white Afrikaner church, and an informal
outdoor black church. This reflected her desire to learn from all religious traditions just like in my country
religion is also considered as a powerful tool associated with women.


Noah's birth was considered a crime, as his mother was black and his father was white. He recounts how
his parents had to hide their relationship and how his mother risked her life to give birth to him. Noah also
discusses the complexities of his mixed-race identity and how he navigated the racial divisions in South Africa
The book provides historical context about apartheid and how it impacted Noah's upbringing. He explains that
the atrocities of apartheid were not taught with an emotional or moral dimension in South African schools, and
his story aims to fill that gap in the colonial period.


In conclusion, I find this story relates to the colonial period In Kenya where whites used to discriminate
against Africans because of their color by having their own schools, churches, and residences.
